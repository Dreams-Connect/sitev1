Join us for a discussion about raising antiracist kids with author of the new book, AntiRacist Baby, Ibram X.Kendi in conversation with Derecka Purnell. A new uprising across the country demanding racial justice is a powerful reminder that families of all backgrounds need to be pro-active in raising children to understand racism and discrimination, and helping our kids to be a force for anti-racist change in the world. How do families raise actively anti-racist children? AntiRacist Baby written by Ibram X. Kendi; illustrated by Ashley Lukashevsky (Kokila Books; on sale June 16, 2020; ages 0-3) Early review of AntiRacist Baby from Publishers Weekly: “Boldly outlined, inclusive illustrations by Lukashevsky complement the text by showing a world populated by people of various skin tones, sizes, identities and orientations, religions, and abilities….[A] mindful companion for families striving together toward a more equitable future.” For readers of Woke Baby by Mahogany L. Browne and Baby Feminists by Libby Babbott-Klein, AntiRacist Baby communicates a critically important idea and intellectual framework in a way that is accessible and empowering for the next generation of changemakers. Take your first steps with AntiRacist Baby! Or rather, follow AntiRacist Baby's nine easy steps for building a more equitable world.
